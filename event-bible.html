We run Pool Play → Bracket — the FIBA / Olympic standard. It guarantees every team multiple games and still crowns a clean champion. Pure single-elimination is the wrong call: too many teams pay $250 and go home after one game.

Bracket structure

10 pools of 5

Round-robin within each pool (Pools A–J) across 4 courts. Four games per team. Standings seed all three Day 2 brackets.

Top-seeded bracket

Highest pool finishers compete for the Pervis Polk Cup. Semifinals → championship game on the feature court.

Day 2 · Consolation + Community
Two full brackets

All remaining teams seeded into Consolation and Community brackets. Every team plays Day 2 — no one goes home after pool play.

Station Playbooks

One card per station. Each lead prints their own and works it like a checklist. Volunteers rotate on two shifts — AM 7:00a–1:00p and PM 12:30p–5:30p — with a 30-minute overlap at lunch so nothing drops.

Court Captain
×4 · one per court · all day
  1. Have the NEXT two teams court-side and ready before the current game ends — the single biggest thing that keeps the day on schedule.
  2. Run the coin toss, confirm both rosters, start promptly.
  3. After the whistle: confirm the score with the ref, both captains initial, runner takes the slip to the table.
  4. Reset the court and call the next matchup within 2 minutes.
  5. Flag any injury, dispute, or conduct issue to a Commissioner immediately.
Check-In
×2 · 8:00–8:45a · then floats
  1. Captain checks in at the registration table.
  2. Confirm the $250 entry is paid (or collect it now).
  3. Collect a signed waiver from every player. No waiver = no play.
  4. Hand out wristbands + t-shirts.
  5. Mark PAID + WAIVERS + CHECKED IN on the master sheet.
  6. Tell them their pool and first game.
Scorekeeper / Table
×2 · all day · the nerve center
  • Take the score slip after every game; log it to the master standings within minutes.
  • Keep pool boards (A–D) current so captains and teams always see the picture.
  • At lunch, hand finished pool standings to the Commissioners for seeding.
  • Post and update both bracket boards as winners advance.
  • Custody of all waivers, rosters, and the master check-in sheet.
Setup / Facilities
×2–3 · 7:00–8:00a & 5:10p+
  • Set the 4 courts, table, banner, PA, water and first-aid stations (see Section 08).
  • Tape pool / bracket boards and court number signs.
  • Mid-day: refill water/ice, empty trash, keep walkways clear.
  • Teardown: trash out, gear packed, school property returned and spaces left clean.
  • Photographer (optional) shadows the feature court & sponsor moments.

10The Game
Rules of Play

Played under the official FIBA 3×3 ruleset — the same fast format used in the Olympic Games — with a few local adaptations noted throughout. Every player, coach, and team rep is responsible for knowing these. Registration is your agreement to abide by them and by the decisions of the referees and Commissioners.

01 The Basics
Players on court3 v 3, +2 subs (5 max)
CourtHalf-court, single hoop · 4 courts
BallOfficial 3×3 ball (size 6)
EligibilityOpen · 18+ (U-18 by approval)
Officials4 refs · Commissioners final
03 Scoring
Made basket inside the arc1
Made basket behind the arc2
Made free throw1
5-on-5 players note: a "three" counts as 2 and everything else as 1. Plan your shot selection.
02 Game Length & How to Win

A game ends when a team first reaches 21 points OR when 10 minutes of game clock have elapsed — whichever comes first. The clock stops on dead balls and free throws as officials direct. In pool play, games may run on a continuous clock if the schedule requires — Commissioners announce the format before play. Reach 21 before time and you win immediately.

Overtime

Tie at the end of regulation → overtime. Teams reset briefly; the team that started on defense gets first possession. First to score 2 points wins — no 10-minute cap in OT.

04 Possession, Check-Ball & Restarts
Coin toss: a toss (or quick shoot-for-it) decides first possession. The winner takes first possession of the game OR of a potential overtime — not both.
Start of game: begins with a check-ball at the top behind the arc, not a jump ball.
After every made basket: the team scored on takes the ball; a defender checks it live; the offense must clear player and ball behind the arc before attacking.
Clearing: after a defensive rebound or steal, the ball must be taken behind the arc before a shot attempt counts.
Check-ball on stoppages: when play stops with no clear control, the team last on defense gets the check-ball.
05 Fouls & Free Throws
Foul on a shot inside the arc1 free throw
Foul on a shot behind the arc2 free throws
Shooting foul + made basketBasket + 1 FT
Team fouls 1–6Check-ball to offense
Team fouls 7, 8, 9 (bonus)2 FT · no possession
Team foul 10+2 FT + possession
Technical foul1 FT + possession
Unsportsmanlike foul2 FT + possession
Bonus (penalty) starts at the 6th team foul. The 10th and every foul after is treated as a technical.
06 Shot Clock & Stalling
  • 12-second shot clock — a shot must hit the rim within 12 seconds of gaining possession.
  • No visible clock on a court? Refs use a verbal/visual count; Commissioners announce the method.
  • Failure to attempt in time = turnover; ball to the other team via check-ball.
07 Subs & Timeouts
  • Subs only on a dead ball with the clock stopped; the incoming player enters after the outgoing steps off behind the end line opposite the basket.
  • One 30-second timeout per team per game, callable by any player on a dead ball.
  • Rosters lock at check-in. A player may appear on only ONE team's roster for the day.

11The Game
Code of Conduct

Pervis Polk mentored generations of Pasadena athletes. This event carries his name, so it carries his standard: we compete hard and we carry ourselves right. These rules are enforced by referees and Commissioners and apply to players, coaches, family, and guests.

09 On the Court
  • Respect every official's call. Referees rule the court; Commissioners have final say. Arguing a call won't reverse it — it costs you.
  • No trash talk targeting a person, family, race, gender, or origin. Competitive fire is welcome; personal attacks are not.
  • No fighting, shoving, or retaliation — ever. Instigator and retaliator can both be removed.
  • Self-officiating spirit: 3×3 moves fast — call your own obvious fouls and play with integrity.
  • Hustle on and off between games to keep the schedule moving — every team's day depends on it.
10 Off the Court & Spectators
  • Treat Blair High School property with care. Clean up after your team.
  • Strictly no alcohol, drugs, tobacco, or vaping anywhere on school grounds.
  • No weapons of any kind.
  • Keep sideline language family-friendly — children are present.
  • Spectators: cheer loud, stay positive. Spectator misconduct can penalize or remove a player's team.

17Promote, Protect & Honor
Safety & Contingency

Safety first, always. Play is at each participant's own risk and a signed waiver must be on file before anyone steps on the court. Commissioners may pause, shorten, or postpone play in an emergency.

First Aid
At the table

First-aid kit and ice live at the tournament table. Report any injury to staff immediately — don't wait.

Hydration
Drink up

July in Pasadena is hot. Water stations provided; players bring their own bottle. Watch teammates for heat illness.

Emergency
Call 911

For any serious injury, call 911, then a Commissioner. Know the nearest exit and the gym address for dispatch.

Contingency plans
If this happens…Do this
Injury on courtRef stops play. First-aid responder + a Commissioner attend. Log it. Resume only when the court is clear and safe.
Extreme heat in the gymIncrease water breaks, slow the cadence, open doors / run fans or AC. Commissioners may add rest between rounds.
Power / facility issuePause, notify the Blair facilities contact, switch to verbal shot-clock counts, consolidate onto working courts.
Under 16 teams registerDrop to the published Mild (12) or Strong (14) format — both pool → bracket. Minimum to run is 12.
Schedule slips behindCourt captains tighten resets to 2 min, shift pool games to a continuous clock, compress the lunch break.
A referee is a no-showA Commissioner or trained court captain covers; rotate one ref across two adjacent courts until covered.
Conduct / fight incidentRefs separate; a Commissioner rules per Section 11. Eject if warranted; involve security/police for safety.
Insurance & venue: confirm event coverage and any Blair High / Pasadena Unified requirements before the day, and keep proof of insurance with the master file at the table.

Awards & Ceremony

The bookends of the day belong to Pervis. The MC runs both moments from a script so nothing is missed. Bracketed cues are for the MC; keep it warm, keep it moving.

9:00a · Opening Ceremony
Tip-off the day
  1. Welcome. "Good morning, Pasadena — welcome to the 1st Annual Pervis Polk Memorial 3×3. [Thank the venue, sponsors, and families.]"
  2. Why we're here. "Pervis poured into this community. Today we play hard, lift each other up, and give back in his name."
  3. Family tribute. [Invite a Polk family member for a 2-minute word.] Then: "Let's take a moment of silence for Pervis." [Pause ~15s.]
  4. Rules briefing. [Captains in. Cover: 21 or 10 min, 12-sec clock, check-ball, conduct, hustle between games. Point to refs & Commissioners.]
  5. Play ball. "Captains, find your courts. Pool play starts at 9:20. Let's run it for Pervis!"
4:50p · Awards & Close
Crown & give back
  1. Gather center court. [Bring in both finalists, families, sponsors.]
  2. Runner-up. [Recognize the runner-up; present runner-up cash.]
  3. Champion. [Trophy sponsor presents the Pervis Polk Cup + champion cash on the mic.]
  4. The Pervis Polk Award. [Name the player who best embodied his spirit — leadership, sportsmanship, lifting teammates.]
  5. The gift. [Present three $1,000 checks to the schools — ideally the Polk family hands them over. Photo.]
  6. Close. "Thank you for running it for Pervis. Leave it better than you found it. See you next year." [Final photo.]
