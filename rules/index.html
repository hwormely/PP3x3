How the Pervis Polk Cup is played, and the standard every player is held to. Games are FIBA-style 3×3 — fast, half-court, physical.

We play to 17. "Cal Tech 17."

Every game ends at 17 points — a nod to a pickup game Pervis Polk and co-founder Ron Wilson once played at Caltech, directly across the street from the Pasadena Poly courts where they hooped for years. That day, games happened to end at 17 — an unusual number in pickup culture, where games usually go to 7, 9, 11, or 21. The two never forgot it and called it "Cal Tech 17." Every Cup game honors that memory. The championship game is the lone exception, played to the traditional 21.

01 — Format

  • Olympic-style FIBA 3×3: half-court, one hoop, played on two half-courts.
  • Inside the arc = 1 point; behind the arc = 2 points.
  • 12-second shot clock. Ball is "checked" (cleared behind the arc) on every change of possession.
  • Teams of five: three players on the court plus two substitutes.

02 — Game Length

A game ends when a team reaches 17 points ("Cal Tech 17") or when 8 minutes of play have elapsed — whichever comes first. If the score is tied at the end of regulation, the first team to score wins.

The championship game is played to 21 points (or the 8-minute cap), as the single exception to Cal Tech 17.

03 — The Two Days

DayFormat
Saturday, Aug 15Pool play — 10 pools of 5 (A–J), full round-robin. Every team plays 4 games — guaranteed. 100 pool games, 8:00 AM–8:30 PM.
Sunday, Aug 16Three single-elimination brackets — Championship, Consolation, and Community — so all 50 teams keep playing into Day 2. Crowns the first Pervis Polk Cup champion.

04 — Seeding Out of Pool Play

Teams are seeded 1–50 by, in order: pool finish → total wins → head-to-head → capped point differential (±10 per game) → total points scored → coin toss.

05 — Eligibility

  • Open division, ages 16 and older, male and female players.
  • Players who are 16 or 17 must have a parent or guardian sign the liability waiver and receive committee approval before playing.
  • Play only for the team you are rostered on. No roster fraud or playing under another name.

06 — Conduct

Every player signs the Code of Conduct at registration. In short:

  • Respect opponents, teammates, referees, staff, and the facility. Compete with an edge, not with cruelty.
  • Referee and Commissioner decisions are final. Disputes go through your captain to the scorer's table.
  • Zero tolerance for fighting, threats, slurs, or intentional attempts to injure — immediate ejection, no refund.
  • Blair High School is a drug-, alcohol-, and tobacco-free campus. No exceptions.

07 — Prizes

  • Cash prizes to the Champion ($1,250), Runner-Up ($750), and 3rd Place ($500).
  • All four podium teams — Champion, Runner-Up, 3rd Place, and Consolation Champion — receive hats and free admission to the next Cup.
  • The Champion takes home the Pervis Polk Cup.

Full rules, seeding key, and conduct standards are in the Event Bible and the printable Rules & Conduct PDF.
