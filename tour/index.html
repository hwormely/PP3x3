GRASSROOTS
TO GLOBAL

The Polk Cup is a vertically integrated 3×3 basketball system — from the neighborhood court to the world stage. Built for the hoop enthusiast on the court and the family around it, at FIBA-level production. We start in Pasadena. We're going everywhere.

"We play to 17." — the Cal Tech 17 signature, born from a pickup game Pervis Polk and Ron Wilson never forgot.

The Thesis

The AAU Alternative

AAU is expensive, travel-heavy, and increasingly resented by families. 3×3 is cheaper, faster, and local-first. Our grassroots tier can rival — and in many families' cases, replace — AAU.

Family + Hoops

FIBA's World Tour is streetball-serious and points-chasing. We keep the elite production but design for families off-court and enthusiasts on it. That's the open lane.

A Real Pathway

Grassroots → regional → "pro." 3×3 is perfectly built for a development ladder. We position as a feeder that funnels talent into FIBA's Olympic system — not a competitor to it.

The Roadmap
Phase 1 · Year One · Pasadena Proof

Year One

Prove the product, then the spectacle.
  • Aug 15–16, 2026 — Inaugural. Blair High School · 50 teams, two days. Bare-bones: perfect gameplay + operations.
  • Winter (~Feb/March 2027) — Event #2. Bare-bones. Absorb learnings; measure player, brand, and fan appetite.
  • Spring 2027 — Event #3. Festival at Pasadena City Hall — vendors, DJ, emcee, musical guests, brand activations.
  • Start of Summer 2027 — Event #4. Festival at the Rose Bowl — the year-one showcase.
  • What it proves: repeatable operations, a family + hoop-head audience, and real sponsor demand.
Phase 2 · National Expansion

Go National

A nationwide circuit — like the ATP tour, for 3×3.
  • Multi-city tour with a season, rankings, and a national final.
  • Grassroots feeder tiers as a genuine AAU alternative for families.
  • Development feeder into FIBA's system — friendly, not competitive.
  • Revenue: sponsorship & activations, media/streaming, merch, vendor & host-city fees.
  • What it proves: the model scales and attracts major brand + investor capital.
Phase 3 · International

Go Global

Bring infrastructure where 3×3 is already loved.
  • Expand abroad, where 3×3 is more established but lacks Polk Cup production + infrastructure.
  • Funded at this phase by sovereign & strategic capital partners.
  • Global calendar; a talent pipeline from local courts to the international stage.
  • What it proves: a durable, global, vertically integrated 3×3 property.

Our Niche

Hoop enthusiasts on the court. Families around the court. FIBA-level production — without the streetball-only, points-chasing intensity of the World Tour.

The Polk Cup = family-friendly + high production + a real grassroots→pro pathway.

Why Now

3×3 Is Rising

An Olympic sport since 2020, low-cost, fast, made for phones and festivals. Globally respected — and under-built in the U.S. The infrastructure gap is the opportunity.

Year One De-Risks It

Four Pasadena events prove operations and demand before national scale. Investors and brands buy a proven engine, not a pitch. That's the plan.
