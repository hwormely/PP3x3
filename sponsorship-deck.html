Honoring Pervis.
Building a tradition.

Pervis Polk spent his life pouring into Pasadena athletes — the mentor who showed up, who believed in kids first, and who taught that how you carry yourself matters as much as how you compete. This two-day, Olympic-style 3×3 tournament carries his name and his standard.

Entry fees fund the event.
Sponsorship powers the gift.

A field of 50 teams at $250 each generates $12,500 in entry revenue — enough to run a first-class two-day tournament. Sponsorship pushes the total to $22,500 and makes the community gift possible.

Every dollar raised puts $1,000 into three local high schools — with John Muir's gift seeding a scholarship fund in Pervis's name.

Goal, year one: $10,000 in sponsorship and grow it from there.
